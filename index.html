    Materials Used

    Nallennai
    Nallennai Details

    Parts Used :
    Extracted from sesame seeds through cold pressing or traditional oil extraction methods.

    Benefits:
    Nallennai is rich in antioxidants, vitamin E, and essential fatty acids, making it highly nourishing for skin and hair. It deeply penetrates tissues, improves blood circulation, strengthens bones, and supports joint health. It helps moisturize dry skin, prevents premature aging, and protects against environmental damage. Sesame oil is also known for its warming property, which helps relieve muscle stiffness and promotes relaxation.

    Why Used:
    Sesame oil is commonly used as a base oil in herbal preparations because of its excellent absorption capacity. It carries herbal properties deep into the body, enhancing therapeutic effectiveness. Its stability and natural preservative qualities help maintain the potency of infused herbs, making it ideal for massage oils and medicinal formulations.

    Vilakkennai
    Vilakkennai Details

    Parts Used:
    Usually a blend of plant-based oils prepared with various herbal extracts and medicinal ingredients.

    Benefits:
    Vilakkennai improves blood circulation, relaxes muscles, and reduces body fatigue. It helps soothe inflammation, supports joint flexibility, and protects skin from dryness. The herbal infusion enhances therapeutic action by combining multiple medicinal properties. It may also help relieve stress and promote physical relaxation.

    Why Used:
    This oil is used as a supporting base in herbal formulations to enhance healing effects. Its blended nature allows different herbs to dissolve effectively, ensuring balanced delivery of nutrients and medicinal compounds. It also helps improve the spreadability of the oil during massage, making it suitable for therapeutic applications.

    Erukkan Ilai
    Erukkan Ilai Details

    Parts Used:
    Leaves of the Calotropis gigantea plant.

    Benefits:
    Erukkan Ilai is traditionally used to relieve body pain, swelling, and inflammation. It contains compounds that help relax muscles and reduce joint stiffness. The leaf is also known for its warming effect, which improves blood flow to affected areas and helps ease discomfort. It has mild analgesic properties that help reduce pain naturally.

    Why Used:
    It is included in herbal oils mainly for pain relief and anti-inflammatory benefits. When infused into oil, its active compounds become easier to apply externally, allowing deeper penetration into muscles and joints. This makes it especially useful for massage oils aimed at relieving physical strain and improving mobility.

    Step-by-step Preparation

    Step 1: Clean & Prepare Leaves
    • Wash all leaves/herbs thoroughly.
    • Remove dirt and dry gently.
    Step 2: Crush Leaves & Herbs
    • Crush each leaf/herb separately using a mortar and pestle.
    • Ensure all leaves are fully submerged in oil.

    Step 3: Mix with Oils
    • Take Nallennai and Vilakkennai in a clean bowl.
    • Add crushed herbs/leaves to the oils.
    • Heat lightly on low flame to release herbal properties.
    • Stir gently for 5-10 minutes.
    Step 4: Cool & Store Oil
    • Let the oil cool to room temperature.
    • Strain to remove solids if needed.
    • Store in a clean, dry glass bottle.
